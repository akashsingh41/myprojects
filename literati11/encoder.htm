There are few things that are more tantalizing than a fast paced adrenaline-pumping coding-race, where coders’ skills are put to test, within a challenging time limit. The online coding strife has always got better and tougher each year. If you think coding is fun or you take it as serious business; then this is the place where you would love to be. In association with our programming partner, CodeChef we bring youEncoder, an online programming contest; the 5 hours long challenge that will take you through the roller coaster ride full of TLA, WA and, the one, ACCEPTED. The experts have hefty prizes to fight for and the beginners can get a good experience about the world of programming contests. So step right up, folks and be ready to get challenged.


About CodeChef:

CodeChef, A "Directi" initiative, is India's first non-commercial platform to promote programming through online coding competition, which are held monthly in more than 35 different programming languages. It not only provides an arena for practice and learning but also gives the programmers a chance to compete with their peers from all over the corners.

Register yourself here for the event here.

The event will be held on 26 January,2011 from 2:00 P.M. to 7:00 P.M.

General Rules:

1. Maximum 2 participants per team, team members can be from different colleges.
2. Each participant should be the member of one and only one team.
3. The submissions should be only from one member of the team(team leader). The submissions from other members of the team will not be entertained and can lead to disqualification of the team.
4. One team can make as many attempts/submissions as possible (there is a penalty for every rejected run by the judge). However, only the result of the last submitted solution will be taken into account.
5. Submitting same code by more than one team will lead to disqualification of teams.
6. The organizers can add, modiy or remove the above mentioned rules. The decision of the organizers will be final.

Rules for Ranking:


1. Users will receive one point for solving a problem (passing all test cases - no partial credit), regardless of the level of difficulty of that problem.
2. Users are ranked according to the most problems solved. Ties will be broken by the total time for each user in ascending order of time.
3. The total time is the sum of the time consumed for each problem solved. The time consumed for a solved problem is the time elapsed from the beginning of the contest to the submittal of the first accepted run plus 20 penalty minutes for every previously rejected run for that problem. There is no time consumed for a problem that is not solved.

The event will be held on 26 January,2011 from 2:00 P.M. to 7:00 P.M.
