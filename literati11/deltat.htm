“Nothing in life is certain except death, taxes and the second law of thermodynamics.”

-Seth Lloyd

Second law of thermodynamics as it states “No process is possible whose sole result is the transfer of heat from a body of lower temperature to a body of higher temperature”, We here ask you to come up with an innovative and feasible mechanism for changing the temperature of the fluids.
A lot of energy and time is spent in modern manufacturing industries, either increasing or decreasing the temperatures of industrial fluids. Delta-t here provides a platform to future engineers to experiment, work out and come up with feasible ideas to .
Stimulate your brain cells, dig deeper, think harder because the event wouldn’t be as easy as it may sound.
Come…Design…Create….and Win!!!!

PROBLEM STATEMENT:

The event includes building of a device/mechanism to raise the temperature of 1L tap water at room temperature to the maximum possible. A maximum of 15 minutes will be allowed to engage the designed device to raise the temperature of water.

With the prime objective of temperature change, importance would also be given to feasibility and novelty of design, the cost involved and the time taken for heating the fluid.

Volume of water initially must not change at the end of process or marks would be deducted.


RULES AND REGULATIONS:

1. Hazardous chemicals like dynamite, fireworks, mercuric chloride, white phosphorous, nitro cellulose, aluminum alkyls, gunpowder and other explosive chemicals should not be used for the temperature change.
2. Poisonous gas like Hydrogen Cyanide, fluorine and chlorine etc should not be emitted from the device/mechanism during the event.
3. Short listing for the final event will be based on the synopsis submitted by the team. The synopsis should include the diagram (if necessary), material and design specifications.
4. Judges decision would be final.
Participants are requested to e-mail their a synopsis of proposed mechanism to deltat.literati11@gmail.com at the earliest so that required changes could be suggested to further improve their model.

Last date of submission of synopsis: 19 Jan 2011


TEAM SPECIFICATIONS

• Maximum of three members per team.
• Cross college teams may also participate in the event.
• The team members should produce a valid college identity card.
• One student cannot be part of two teams.
• Number of entries per team is not restricted.
JUDGING CRITERION

A ‘Delta-t Quotient’ will be calculated for each team on the basis of the formula given: Delta-t Quotient = (∆T2.N)/C.t
1. Feasibility and novelty of the design(N)
2. Temperature change(∆T)
3. Time required for the change(t)
4. Cost involved(C)

FAQ’S

1) Is direct heating using coils or metallic strip allowed ?
Ans. NO,direct use of fire or electricity is not allowed to heat the water during the phase of temperature change.
